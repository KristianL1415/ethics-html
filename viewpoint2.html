Intermediate Viewpoint

When can a Search be Performed?

With a warrant provided to a law-enforcement officer by the courts.

When a reasonable amount of probable cause exists, e.g. contraband in 'plain-sight'

What Constitutes a 'Search'?

Courts state a 'reasonable expectation of privacy' must be present for a 'search' to have occurred.

Open-Field Doctrine

Encrypted vs. Unencrypted Data

Two basic types of data being sent over the Internet

Encrypted data cannot be easily read when intercepted

Stakeholders

The United States government might be against this type of policy.

The general public will also likely be split on this issue, but would prefer it over full surveillance.

Surveillance development companies will lose some business, and therefore would likely be against this.

Terrorists may be able to take advantage by increasing their use of encryption.

Conclusion

Encrypted data should be protected and require a warrant to search. Unecrypted data does not provide an expectation of privacy, and therefore should not require a warrant to search.
