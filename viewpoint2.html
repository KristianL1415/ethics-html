Intermediate Viewpoint

Expectation of Privacy

Courts state an expectation of privacy must be present for a 'search' to have occurred.

Ex. Open-Field Doctrine

Encrypted vs. Unencrypted Data

Two basic types of data being sent over the Internet

Encrypted data cannot be easily read when intercepted

Conclusion

Encrypted data should be protected and require a warrant to search. Unecrypted data does not provide an expectation of privacy, and therefore should not require a warrant to search.
