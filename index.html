Ethical Dilemma

Should the United States Government be allowed to use surveillance technology? And if so to what extent?

Research Methodologies

  • Google Search
  • The Homeland Security Library
  • Cornell Law Website

Most of our resources were found online because widespread surveillance is a fairly recent issue.

Stakeholders Involved

  • United States Government - wants to govern and protect its citizens and the nation as a whole. Willing to stop acts of terror even at a high cost.
  • People in the United States - U.S. Citizens want to be protected from terrorist threats, however they also want their right to privacy respected.
  • People Outside of the United States - Even people outside of the United States have been spyed on by the U.S. Government, so they will have similar concerns as people in the country.
  • Surveillance Technology Development Companies - Companies who develop surveillance technology will depend on governments being able to use their technology. They will push for surveillance.
  • Terrorist Organizations - Terror groups will find that committing acts of terror may become easier or more difficult depending on how far the government is willing and able to go to stop them.
